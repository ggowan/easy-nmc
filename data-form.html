National Ministries Allocation Data Form

Please use this form to report your parish's financial information for the National Ministries Allocation Process. The due date for completing this form is May 15, 2015. Please bear in mind that the form must be reviewed and approved by the parish priest, president, treasurer, and internal audit committee chairperson by that date.

You may share this form by sending the complete URL to another person. Please note that the URL contains a randomized key that allows the recipient to view the form and modify its contents.

In order to edit the form, please click the pencil button in the top-right corner. It should turn green to indicate you are editing the form. Only one person will be able to edit the form at a time. Your changes will be automatically saved as you type, as long as there is a working internet connection. A red error message will appear if the connection is lost or the data is not being saved.

Please follow these steps to verify that this form is working correctly on your web browser:

  1. Click the pencil button to enter editing mode (it should turn green).
  2. Make a small change to one of the fields in the form.
  3. Refresh the page in your browser.
  4. Verify your changes are still visible. If so, that means the form is working correctly and your changes are being saved.

If the form does not seem to be working, try using a newer web browser, such as Google Chrome. You may also contact Geoffrey Gowan for assistance at geoffrey.gowan@gmail.com or (510) 230-7650.

C6. Capital Improvement Projects

Please report deductible capital improvement expenses below in accordance with the following guidelines:

  • The expenses must have been for projects of significant cost and lasting value, such as land purchases, facility construction, iconography, and interior decor (e.g. pews, iconostasion, proskinitaria, etc.).
  • As with all deductions, this deduction only applies for amounts that were included in parish gross expenses (section B, above). If you did not report the capital improvement as an expense, do not take the deduction.
  • For loan payments, see sections C7 and C8 below.
  • Do not deduct an aggregation of small maintenance expenses that do not significantly extend the life of the facility or its major components, or that can be expected to occur on a regular basis every year.

C7. Construction Loan Interest

Please report interest paid on construction loans below.

Construction loans are short-term, interest-only loans in which the balance increases as payments are made on an ongoing construction project. If your parish's loan requires fixed monthly payments over a period of years and the balance is going down, not up, it is a mortgage and should be reported in section C8 below.

20132014

C8. Mortgage Payments

Please report the deductible portion of your parish's mortgage payments below. Payments on a mortgage are deductible to the extent they are included in the gross expenses reported in section B, above. For example,

  • If you reported only the interest portion of your mortgage payments as an expense in section B, then only the interest portion is deductible.
  • If you included both interest and principal in section B, both are deductible.
20132014

C9. Fundraising Expenses

Please report deductible fundraising expenses below, in accordance with the following guidelines:

  • The expenses must have been directly caused by a fundraising activity, such that if the fundraising activity had not been undertaken, the expenses would not have been incurred.
  • Expenses associated with the regular parish stewardship program are not deductible.
  • Do not deduct a portion of regular parish expenses such as the priest's salary or facility maintenance as fundraising expenses.
  • Expenses associated with rental property or businesses operated in parish facilities should not be considered part of the parish and should be excluded from parish gross expenses. However, in order to be excluded, such expenses must actually be measured separately from other parish expenses.
  • As with all deductions, this deduction only applies for amounts that were included in parish gross expenses (section B, above).
  • Utility expenses for fundraising events may be deducted only if they were actually measured apart from regular parish usage.

Expenses that are typically deducted in this section cover food, materials, rental expenses, and special labor for events such as festivals, dinner dances, and golf outings.

C10. Greek School and Day School Expenses

Please report deductible Greek School and Day School expenses below, in accordance with the following guidelines:

  • Expenses must be directly attributable to the operation of the school.
  • Expenses that are typically deducted include teacher salaries, payroll taxes, insurance, equipment, supplies, and books. Other expenses may be deductible if they are directly attributable to the program.
  • Day School expenses are deductible if the school operates 5 days a week for at least 3 hours each day.
  • As with all deductions, this deduction only applies for amounts that were included in parish gross expenses (section B, above).
20132014

C11. Religious Education Expenses

Please report deductible expenses for religious education below, in accordance with the following guidelines:

  • Sunday School, catechism classes, lectures and seminars are all examples of religious education programs that may have deductible expenses.
  • The parish priest's salary is never a deductible expense.
  • Payments to other teachers or visiting speakers may be deducted.
  • Any other expense directly attributable to the program may be deducted, such as payroll taxes, insurance, equipment, supplies, books, etc.
